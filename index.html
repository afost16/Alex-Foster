Hi! I am a qualitative researcher at the R-House Robotics Lab and incoming Master's student at the University of Washington's Human-Centered Design & Engineering program.

I recently graduated with my Bachelor's in Informatics with a focus in Human-Computer Interaction at Indiana University. My research in human-robot interaction (HRI) and human-computer interaction (HCI) has helped researchers better conduct research for people living with dementia and their carepartners. I am particularly interested in studying communities and subcultures like these that need attention in this digital era. Ultimately, I want to develop timeless systems and methods that serve as counterbalances to rapid technological advancement so people can live healthier lives with technology.

I am currently working on a project to detect changes in speech for people living with dementia and mild cognitive impairment using a conversational robot at the R-House Robotics Lab, in collaboration with researchers at DePaul University. When I'm not researching or doing schoolwork, I enjoy weightlifting, bouldering, cooking vegan food, and watching my favorite soccer team, Manchester United.
